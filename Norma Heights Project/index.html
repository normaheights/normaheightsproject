Norma Heights (1885–1963)

Overview: Norma Heights was an influential American activist, educator, and writer, best known for her pivotal role in the women’s suffrage movement. Revered for her charisma, strategic mind, and dedication to justice, Norma was a key figure in the fight for the 19th Amendment, which granted women the right to vote in 1920. Her legacy extends beyond suffrage, encompassing labor reform, education, and international women’s rights.

Legacy
